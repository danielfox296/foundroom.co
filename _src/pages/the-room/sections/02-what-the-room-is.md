## A room. Not a program.

Most founder communities are built around the company. Deals, tactics, intros, growth. Those things have their place.

The Right Room is built around the founder. Specifically: the founder who has built something real and is now facing a disruption that most founders don't talk about.

You built the company on a set of values. Not values you necessarily examined. Values inherited from culture, family, the startup ecosystem's received wisdom about what success means. The company worked as long as those values produced returns. The exit, or the moment before it when growth flattens, is when that covenant breaks. The old values stop working. The next direction isn't visible yet.

The work in this room is surfacing which values are actually yours, and which were accepted without examination. Not to change anything. To make the choice conscious. Once you've named what you actually want, the tradeoffs become clear. The decision is yours to count.

Two live conversations per month. One member essay or audio reflection. Occasional paired dialogues. Invitations to retreats. A small cohort, selected carefully.

The promise is not constant engagement. The promise is a serious room where you can bring the actual question, not the version that's easier to say out loud.

Annual membership. No performance required. No pitch. No scoreboard.

$6,000 per year. Founding member rate: $5,400.
