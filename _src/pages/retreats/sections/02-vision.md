## A conversation founders almost never have.

There is a particular kind of conversation that founders almost never have.

Not about the company. Not about the market, the team, the next raise, or the exit multiple. About the actual experience of building something — what it takes, what it costs, what it means, and what they want from the years ahead.

These conversations don't happen at conferences. They don't happen in masterminds. They barely happen between co-founders, because there's too much at stake in those relationships to be fully honest.

They happen when a small group of people who've been somewhere real get out of their usual context, slow down enough to think, and find themselves in a room where performance isn't required.

That's what Found Room retreats are designed to be.
