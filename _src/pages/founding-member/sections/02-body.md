<p>Found Room is a private annual membership for founders who have built something real and found that the arrival didn't produce what they expected. Not a coaching program. Not a mastermind. A room with peers who are in the same territory and don't need anything from each other.</p>

<p>The founding cohort is the first group through the door. It's smaller than what Found Room will eventually become, and founding members help shape what the community is. The rate is set below the standard annual price. It stays that rate for as long as you're a member.</p>

<div class="section-break" markdown="1"></div>

<h2>What membership includes</h2>

<p>Most founders who have built something real have been running on a set of values they didn't choose. Inherited from culture, family, social pressure, the received wisdom about what success means in the startup ecosystem. The work of Found Room is surfacing which of those values are actually yours.</p>

<p>The Right Room is the ongoing private community where that work happens. Monthly peer conversations with a small cohort of founders in a similar situation. A space where you can bring the actual question without optimizing it for how it sounds. Access to the full archive of Found Journal writing. Direct contact with Daniel.</p>

<p>The conversations are not about fixing things. They are about clarifying what belongs to you and what was just accepted. About recognizing that you get to choose which values you carry forward, and what the tradeoffs of that choice are. The facilitator doesn't prescribe what to change. That would be overstepping into your own domain. The work is showing you that the choice belongs to you.</p>

<p>Founding members also get first access to Threshold Conversations (private 90-minute sessions) at the founding rate, and first access to Found Room retreats as they come online.</p>

<h2>Who this is for</h2>

<p>Founders who have built something real. Typically bootstrapped or nearly so. Post-exit or still operating but at the point where the mechanism has stopped working the way it used to.</p>

<p>What brings someone here is usually some version of tired, not sure, something isn't working. But the more precise description is this: the company ran on a set of values you haven't examined yet. You weren't running it badly. You were running it hard, probably effectively. But most of what drove the building wasn't examined. It was just the baseline. The machinery of ambition, the pressure to prove something, the idea that success meant a particular kind of arrival.</p>

<p>That machinery worked while it had to. Then the external situation changed. Maybe the company exited. Maybe you stepped back and it runs without you now. Maybe you're still there but you notice you've been solving the same problem for three years and the feeling that used to come with that has shifted. And now you're in a room where you can look at what you were actually doing it for.</p>

<p>The external situation is intact. The internal question is open.</p>

<p>This is not for founders in acute crisis. It's for founders whose lives are broadly working and whose meaning is the problem.</p>

<p>The cohort is kept small because the quality of conversation depends on who is in it. Founding members are chosen for the fit, not the resume.</p>
