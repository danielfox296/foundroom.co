<p>Found Room is a private annual membership for founders who have built something real and found that the arrival didn't produce what they expected. Not a coaching program. Not a mastermind. A room with peers who are in the same territory and don't need anything from each other.</p>

<p>The founding cohort is the first group through the door. It's smaller than what Found Room will eventually become, and founding members help shape what the community is. The rate is set below the standard annual price. It stays that rate for as long as you're a member.</p>

<div class="section-break" markdown="1"></div>

<h2>What membership includes</h2>

<p>The Right Room is the ongoing private community. Monthly peer conversations with a small cohort of founders in a similar situation. Access to the full archive of Found Journal writing. Direct contact with Daniel.</p>

<p>Founding members also get first access to Threshold Conversations (private 90-minute sessions) at the founding rate, and first access to Found Room retreats as they come online.</p>

<h2>Who this is for</h2>

<p>Founders who have built something real. Typically bootstrapped or nearly so. Post-exit or still operating but at the point where the mechanism has stopped working the way it used to. The presenting experience tends to be some version of tired, not sure, something isn't working. The underlying situation is more specific than that, and the essays on this site are probably the best description of it.</p>

<p>This is not for founders in acute external crisis. It's for founders whose external situation is broadly intact and whose internal situation is the question.</p>

<p>The cohort is kept small because the quality of conversation in a room depends on who is in it. Founding members are chosen for the fit, not the resume.</p>
