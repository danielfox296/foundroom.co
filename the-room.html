What The Right Room is

A room. Not a program.

Most founder communities are built around the company. Deals, tactics, intros, growth. Those things have their place.

The Right Room is built around the founder.

Not as a therapeutic project. Not as a self-improvement program. As a recognition that the most consequential variable in any founder's life is the founder themselves — and that variable rarely gets honest attention.

Two live conversations per month. One member essay or audio reflection. Occasional paired dialogues. Invitations to retreats. A small cohort, selected carefully.

The promise is not constant engagement. The promise is a serious room — one where you can bring the actual question, not the version that's easier to say out loud.

Annual membership. No performance required. No pitch. No scoreboard.

$6,000 per year. Founding member rate: $5,400.

What the membership includes.

Monthly conversations

Two live sessions per month. Small group. Real conversation — not a presentation, not a check-in. You can bring the actual question.

Member reflections

Retreats

Founding members are invited to in-person gatherings. Two to four days. A small group. The room becomes real.

Who belongs

The Right Room is for founders who have built something real and are willing to look honestly at themselves as part of what they've built. Pre-exit, post-exit, or continuing to operate. The stage doesn't matter. The willingness does.

The founding cohort is being selected personally. The criteria are not application forms, revenue thresholds, or reputation. The question is whether you're the kind of founder who makes the room better for everyone in it.
