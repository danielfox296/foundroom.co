What this is

A room, not a program.

Found Room is a private community for experienced founders. Not a course. Not a coaching program. Not a mastermind with a curriculum and an outcome promise.

A room where founders can be honest about what they're carrying — and find others who understand what that actually means. Through retreats, peer conversations, and an ongoing community, Found Room creates conditions for the kind of clarity that rarely happens alone.

The members are the point. What they've built, what they're navigating, and who they're becoming.

What founders find here.

Lightness

Less weight carried alone. More ease in decisions, relationships, and the ordinary moments of a day.

Clarity

A clearer sense of what matters, what doesn't, and what you're actually building toward — in work and in life.

The right rooms

Founders who've been somewhere worth going. Honest peers. Conversations that don't require you to perform.

Groundedness

A stable place to stand that doesn't depend on outcomes, valuations, or whether this quarter went well.

Expansion

More of yourself available — for the work, for the people you love, for the life you're actually trying to build.

Progress

Visible, real movement. Not optimized metrics. A life that looks and feels different than it did before.

"The business stops carrying personal weight it was never designed to carry. And something opens up."

Found Room emerged from a simple observation: the most successful founders are often carrying the most — and have the fewest places to set it down honestly.

The community draws on The Identity Cost, a body of work examining how founders route personal needs through business systems — and what becomes possible when they stop. Not as a framework to follow, but as a lens available to those who want it.

Retreats, peer cohorts, and an ongoing private community form the structure. The work is whatever each member brings.

Who belongs here

Founders who've built something real. Pre-exit, post-exit, or continuing to operate. What they share isn't a stage — it's a willingness to look at themselves honestly and invest in a life that matches what they've built.
