Found Room Retreats

Some things can only happen when you leave.

Found Room retreats are designed to create the conditions for honest conversation, genuine rest, and the kind of clarity that doesn't happen at a desk.

The vision

A conversation founders almost never have.

There is a particular kind of conversation that founders almost never have.

Not about the company. Not about the market, the team, the next raise, or the exit multiple. About the actual experience of building something — what it takes, what it costs, what it means, and what they want from the years ahead.

These conversations don't happen at conferences. They don't happen in masterminds. They barely happen between co-founders, because there's too much at stake in those relationships to be fully honest.

They happen when a small group of people who've been somewhere real get out of their usual context, slow down enough to think, and find themselves in a room where performance isn't required.

That's what Found Room retreats are designed to be.

Why in-person

Something about leaving matters.

Not just the change of scenery. The discontinuity. The physical act of stepping out of the environment where you perform your usual roles. The absence of your usual routines, your usual responsibilities, your usual self.

In that space, different things become possible. Questions that felt too large to hold at a desk become manageable. Conversations that would feel risky in a familiar context feel natural. Rest that wouldn't come at home arrives on its own.

Found Room retreats are designed around that discontinuity. Not as a vacation — as a genuine container for the work that the rest of life doesn't make room for.

Found Room Retreats — somewhere else entirely

What we're building.

Small

Intimate enough that everyone in the room knows everyone else by the end. Not a conference. Not a cohort of fifty. A room where real conversation is possible.

Honest

Structured around genuine exchange, not performance. No keynotes. No panels. No one selling anything.

Located

In places that earn the trip. Environments chosen for their capacity to create the right kind of space — natural, removed, genuinely beautiful.

Unhurried

Long enough to actually land somewhere. Not a weekend of back-to-back sessions. Time to think, to walk, to sit with what comes up.

Who these are for

Founders who've built something real and want to invest in the full picture. Pre-exit founders carrying a load they rarely set down. Post-exit founders navigating the disorientation of what comes next. Founders who are continuing to operate and want to do it with more clarity, more ease, and more of themselves available.

You don't need to be in crisis. You don't need to have a specific problem to solve. You need to be the kind of founder who believes that investing in yourself is at least as important as investing in your company.
